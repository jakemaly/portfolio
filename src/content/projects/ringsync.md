RingSync was designed as a response to a critical gap in emergency pre-hospital monitoring: current devices measure vitals from a single site, which can miss life-threatening asymmetric conditions like aortic dissection. The system uses two compact rings placed on each index finger to simultaneously capture blood pressure, oxygen saturation, skin temperature, and ECG data from both arms.

The dual-ring design enables bilateral comparison in real time — a paramedic can immediately see blood pressure differences between arms on a tablet dashboard, flagging conditions that would otherwise go undiagnosed until the hospital. A guided 12-lead ECG workflow reconstructs clinical-standard cardiac readings through four quick electrode positions, eliminating the need for bulky chest pads.

The entire system — both rings and charging case — fits within a 125×50×45mm footprint and weighs 119g, smaller than a glasses case and lighter than a smartphone. Setup takes approximately 3 minutes, with continuous vitals available immediately upon placement.
